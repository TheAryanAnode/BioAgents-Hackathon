# SynthesisOS — Marketing copy

## Tagline

**Turn literature into hypotheses before your competitors finish the literature review.**

---

## Description

SynthesisOS is an autonomous biomedical research agent that ingests papers from Semantic Scholar, PubMed, and arXiv—or your own uploaded PDFs—and builds a live knowledge graph of genes, pathways, and diseases. It detects **structural gaps** in the literature (open triangles: two concepts that never meet directly but share a mechanistic bridge) and generates testable hypotheses with scored evidence, confidence tracking, and funder-ready investment briefs. Every agent action is streamed to an exportable audit log for regulatory transparency. Built for Track 02 Autonomous Research with a commercial angle: patient stratification, unmet-need scoring, ROI modeling, and PDF reports tailored for healthcare investors and R&D leaders.

---

## Pitch

What if discovering the next drug target didn’t start with a keyword search—it started with what the literature *forgot to connect*?

SynthesisOS deploys a team of AI agents that continuously ingest scientific literature, merge it with your proprietary PDFs, and map the hidden architecture of a research domain. While traditional tools retrieve papers, SynthesisOS **reasons over the graph**: it finds pairs of well-studied concepts that co-occur with a shared pathway but are never directly linked—and turns those gaps into ranked, evidence-backed hypotheses.

For **researchers**, it’s a living synthesis engine: interactive graph, grounded chat with citations, and upload-any-PDF workflow. For **commercial teams**, each hypothesis ships with patient population estimates, competitive whitespace, and ROI breakdowns. For **regulators and compliance**, every API call, embedding, and model invocation is logged and exportable.

We built it to run in the real world: demo mode without an API key, rate-limit-safe Gemini for chat and reports, and a full-stack pipeline that completes in seconds. SynthesisOS doesn’t replace scientists—it gives them a tireless research partner that sees the white space in the graph.

**Ask us to run `autism genomics` live—we’ll show you a hypothesis your search bar would never surface.**
